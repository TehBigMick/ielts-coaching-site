Service IELTS Writing Task 1

IELTS Writing Task 1 coaching

For candidates who lose marks on overview, comparisons, and accuracy (even when grammar is “OK”).

Who it’s for

  • Band 5.5–7.0 candidates who need reliable Task 1 structure
  • People who describe data but don’t compare it properly
  • Anyone losing marks on overview and key trends

Common problems

  • Weak or missing overview sentence
  • Listing numbers instead of comparing trends
  • Wrong units, misreading charts, or unclear quantifiers
  • Organisation that feels “messy” to the examiner

How the approach fixes it

  • Repeatable overview method for each chart type
  • Comparison-first structure (not number dumping)
  • Language upgrades for trends, proportions, and changes
  • Error-proofing: units, time shifts, and selection

Typical improvement

Many candidates improve +0.5 bands in 4–8 weeks when practising weekly and rewriting properly.

Service IELTS Writing Task 2

IELTS Writing Task 2 coaching

For candidates stuck at Band 6–6.5 with “good ideas” but weak development, logic, and control.

Who it’s for

  • Band 6–7 candidates aiming for 7.0+
  • People who get vague feedback (“develop your ideas”)
  • Anyone whose essays feel repetitive, general, or unclear

Common problems

  • Weak position statement and unclear direction
  • Paragraphs that don’t logically build and support a point
  • Examples that are too general or irrelevant
  • Grammar “small errors” that damage clarity and coherence

How the approach fixes it

  • Clear paragraph logic (point → explanation → support)
  • Method for developing ideas (so feedback stops being vague)
  • Sentence-level correction + rewrite cycles
  • Band-descriptor scoring so you know what improved

Typical improvement

Many committed candidates improve +0.5 to +1.0 bands across 6–10 weeks, depending on starting level and practice hours.

Service IELTS Speaking

IELTS Speaking coaching

For candidates who “can speak” but lose marks for repetition, weak support, or low control in Parts 2–3.

Who it’s for

  • Band 6–7 candidates aiming for a stronger, controlled performance
  • People who ramble or go blank under pressure
  • Anyone stuck because answers repeat and lack depth

Common problems

  • Part 2 stories that lack structure or run out of time
  • Part 3 answers that stay too general and unsupported
  • Overusing the same words and phrases
  • Fluency drops when trying to use “advanced” vocabulary

How the approach fixes it

  • Simple answer frameworks that sound natural (not memorised)
  • Support methods: examples, reasons, consequences, comparisons
  • Targeted range: grammar + vocabulary that you can actually control
  • Timed practice with feedback linked to band criteria

Typical improvement

Many candidates improve +0.5 to +1.0 bands over 6–10 weeks with regular recording practice.

Writing correction (no lessons)

Ideal if you want fast, clear improvements through marking and rewrites.

Who it’s for

  • Busy candidates who can’t schedule regular lessons
  • People who need clear corrections and rewrite guidance
  • Candidates who learn best by revising and resubmitting

What you get

  • Band estimate + criteria breakdown
  • Sentence-level corrections (clarity + accuracy)
  • Notes on structure and development
  • Next-step rewrite tasks

How it works

  1. You submit a Task 1 or Task 2 essay.
  2. I mark it against the criteria and correct at sentence level.
  3. You rewrite using the guidance (this is where improvement happens).
  4. Optional: resubmission for re-scoring.
